Staple food

Our Region is found in the Savanna climatic zone where tubers thrive.
Our Staple food is called "Achu". It is made from cocoyams.
After boiling, the Cocoyam is pounded to a smooth consistency in a mortar and tied in plantain leaves.
The soup is made from palm oil and limestone with alot of meat and cow skin.
During special ceremonies like traditional marriages, the achu is eaten with a special soup made from pumpkin seeds.
It is not uncommon to have a glass of freshly tapped palmwine to accompany your achu.
